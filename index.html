About Dr. Vince Cascone

Dr. Vince Cascone has dedicated a career to Catholic education, serving as a classroom teacher, principal for 19 years, and superintendent of Catholic schools in the Archdiocese of Kansas City in Kansas for seven years. This breadth of experience provides a unique understanding of the challenges and opportunities facing Catholic school leaders at every level.

As a principal, Dr. Vince Cascone led his school to a U.S. Department of Education National Blue Ribbon School award. He has also been recognized nationally as both the NAESP National Distinguished Principal of the Year and the NCEA Distinguished Principal of the Year, bringing both proven expertise and a deep passion for developing the next generation of Catholic school leaders.

Holding a Doctorate in Educational Leadership (Ed.D.) and a Master's degree in Educational Administration, Dr. Vince Cascone combines scholarly research with real-world, practical leadership experience. He also serves as an adjunct professor at Benedictine College, teaching a Master of Arts in School Leadership course and forming the next generation of Catholic school leaders.

Our Namesake

Seton Educational Services is named in honor of St. Elizabeth Ann Seton, the first American-born saint and patron saint of Catholic schools. A pioneer in Catholic education, she founded the first free Catholic school in America and established the Sisters of Charity, dedicating her life to forming young minds in faith and knowledge. Her vision and courage continue to inspire Catholic school leaders today, and her legacy is the foundation upon which our mission is built.
