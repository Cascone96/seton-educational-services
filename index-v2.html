The Reality

What Catholic school principals face every day

1

Thrown Into the Deep End

New principals in years 1–2 with insufficient training for the unique demands of Catholic school leadership. The learning curve is steep, the stakes are high, and there's no playbook.

2

Wearing Every Hat

Principal, fundraiser, marketer, HR director, facilities manager — all at once, with no help. The workload is unsustainable, and burnout is always one bad week away.

3

On an Island

No thought partner, no peer network, no one to call when things get hard. Leading a Catholic school can be the loneliest job in education.

What Happens When Nothing Changes

Unprepared New Principal →

Poor early decisions that damage credibility with staff, parents, and pastor

Constantly reactive — putting out fires instead of building systems

The school community loses confidence in leadership

Wearing Every Hat →

High turnover — principals leave within 3–5 years, and the school starts over

The Catholic mission suffers — faith formation gets pushed aside for operations

Good teachers leave — they don't get the support they need from an overwhelmed leader

Isolation →

Blind spots go unchecked — no one to challenge their thinking or offer perspective

Stagnation — the school stays stuck doing what it's always done

The weight becomes unbearable, leading to resignation

What's Really at Stake

The consequences extend far beyond any single school

1

Enrollment Declines

Families sense instability and leave. Once trust erodes, it takes years to rebuild — if the school survives long enough to try.

2

The Diocese Loses Schools

Underprepared, burned out, isolated principals are the #1 reason Catholic schools close. Every closure is a community that loses something irreplaceable.

3

A Generation of Students Is Affected

Every struggling principal means hundreds of kids getting less than they deserve — less formation, less stability, less of the Catholic education their families chose for them.
